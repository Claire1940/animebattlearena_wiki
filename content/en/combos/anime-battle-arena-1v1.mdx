<Section icon={Target} title="Core Mechanics: M1 Mastery and Spacing">

In an elite **anime battle arena 1v1** match, relying on your special skills to start a combo will quickly get you punished by experienced players. Skills should primarily function as combo extenders or guard breakers. Your primary tool for initiating pressure is the basic M1 attack.

### The Power of Raw M1 Attacks
Landing a raw M1 string is the ultimate way to set up high-damage combos. If you can initiate an M1 combo with all of your skills off cooldown, you can easily transition into a 100-to-0 damage sequence. 

### Capitalizing on Jump End Lag
When an opponent double-jumps, they experience a brief moment of "end lag" upon hitting the ground. This slows down their movement and prevents them from immediately responding. Use this window to dash around their block and land a raw M1 combo starter.

### Managing the 5-Hit M1 Limit
You can block up to four consecutive M1 hits from an opponent. However, the fifth M1 hit in a basic sequence is a natural guard break. If you hold your block through all five hits, you will be stunned, leaving you completely open to an extended combo. You must learn to drop your block or dodge (Q) before the fifth hit connects.

| Action | Input | Combat Function |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Basic Strike (M1)** | Mouse1 | Your primary combo starter and pressure tool |
| **Aerial Launcher** | Space + Mouse1 | Launches the opponent into the air for ladder extensions |
| **Dodge / Dash** | Q | Used to escape pressure or reposition during end lag |
| **Block** | F | Defends against basic strikes; vulnerable to guard breaks |

<Callout type="warning" title="Guard Break Warning">
Never hold down block when you see an opponent initiating a known guard-breaking skill. It is far better to take the raw chip damage of the skill than to get guard-broken and suffer a full 100-to-0 follow-up combo.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Map} title="Step-by-Step 1v1 Match Plan">

To climb the ELO ladder consistently, you need a structured game plan for every single match. Follow these steps to systematically dismantle your opponent's defense:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Study and Ban Strategically">
    Analyze the current meta and ban characters that counter your main or have highly evasive kits. If you run a close-range physical build, ban high-tier runners or characters with instant guard breaks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Bait the Dodge (Q)">
    Start the round by playing defensively. Use your sprint (Shift) to weave in and out of the opponent's M1 range. Throw out a safe, low-cooldown projectile to force them to waste their dodge.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Initiate with Raw M1s">
    Once their dodge is on cooldown, look for a jump landing or a missed skill to close the gap. Dash behind them and initiate a Mouse1 string.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Extend and Finish">
    After 3 or 4 basic strikes, launch them into the air (Space + Mouse1) and cycle through your special skills (1-4 keys) to maximize your damage output before they recover.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Success Check">
Always practice these setups in the in-game Training Mode first. Set the dummy to move and attack so you can learn the exact range of your character's M1s and skills.
</Callout>
