<Section icon={Target} title="The Fundamentals of Combo Construction">

To excel in 2026 PvP, you must move beyond simple button mashing. Most **anime battle arena combos** follow a specific structure: Starter, Extension, and Finisher. The most common extension is the "Ladder," where you launch an opponent into the air using an aerial attack and follow them up to continue the M1 string.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The M1 Opener">
    Land 3 to 4 grounded M1 hits. This puts the opponent in hit-stun, preventing them from blocking or dodging immediately. Do not finish the full 5-hit string if you plan to launch them, as the final hit usually causes knockback.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Uptilt (Launch)">
    While the opponent is stunned, press Space and Mouse1 simultaneously. This launches the enemy into the air. This is the critical transition point for all high-damage anime battle arena combos.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Aerial Extension">
    Jump immediately after the launch and continue your M1 string in the air. Depending on your character's gravity and attack speed, you can usually land another 3-4 hits before they fall.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Skill Integration">
    As the opponent starts to fall or at the end of your aerial string, use a special move (1-4). Some moves are specifically designed to "slam" the opponent back to the ground or "snatch" them back into another combo.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Dodge Management">
Never use your dodge (Q) offensively unless you are 100% certain it will lead to a kill. A wasted dodge leaves you completely defenseless against an opponent's full ladder combo.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Competitive PvP Tactics and Defense">

In Ranked 1v1 or 2v2 matches, your ability to perform **anime battle arena combos** is only half the battle. You must also know how to prevent your opponent from doing the same. Defense in ABA is built around the "Neutral Game"—the state where neither player is currently in a combo.

| Defensive Tactic | Best Used When... | Risk Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blocking (F)** | Opponent is using predictable M1 strings | Low (can be guard-broken) |
| **Dodging (Q)** | Opponent uses a high-damage special move | Medium (long cooldown) |
| **Spacing** | Opponent is aggressive and "fishing" for M1s | Low (requires good movement) |
| **Counter-Attacking**| Opponent finishes a move with long recovery | High (requires perfect timing) |

Winning more fights requires a focus on "punishment." When an opponent misses a move like a "guard break" or a heavy finisher, they are stuck in an animation. This is your cue to initiate your most damaging string.

</Callout>
