To execute a high-level **anime battle arena combo guide** strategy, you must learn the "Ladder" technique. This involves hitting the opponent multiple times in the air to prevent them from landing.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Confirm the Opening">
    Land at least two grounded M1s to ensure the opponent is stunned and cannot immediately block.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Launch">
    Perform an "Up-tilt" by jumping and hitting M1 simultaneously. This sends both you and the opponent skyward.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Air M1s">
    While in the air, continue the M1 string. Depending on the character, you can usually land 2-3 hits before they fall.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Skill Connection">
    As the opponent begins to fall, activate a skill (1, 2, 3, or 4) that has a large hitbox or "Auto-aim" properties to finish the damage.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Dodge Warning">

<Section icon={Shield} title="Defensive Maneuvers and Punishment">

A great offense is nothing without a solid defense. In ABA, "Punishing" is the act of attacking an opponent immediately after they miss a move or finish a string on your block.

**Key Defensive Tactics:**
- **Perfect Blocking**: Timing your F-key right as the attack hits can stagger the opponent.
- **Back-Dodging**: Using Q while holding S (Back) to create space during an enemy's skill startup.
- **Jump-Dodging**: Jumping while dodging to avoid low-profile "AoE" (Area of Effect) attacks.

| Move Category | Blockable? | Counter Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard M1s** | Yes | Block the first 3, then Dodge or Counter-M1. |
| **Guard Breaks** | No | You must Dodge (Q) or move out of the hitbox. |
| **Projectiles** | Mostly | Block to reduce damage, or Sprint-dodge to close gap. |
| **Ultimates** | No | Run or use an "I-frame" (Invincibility frame) move. |

<Callout type="info" title="Spacing is Key">
The best defense is not being where the attack lands. Use your Sprint (Shift) to hover just outside the opponent's M1 range. This baits them into swinging and missing, leaving them wide open for your own combo.
</Callout>

When practicing in the training room, turn on the "Hit-stun Indicator" if available. This helps you see exactly when the opponent is able to move again. If the indicator disappears before your next hit connects, your combo is not "True," and a skilled player will escape it every time.
