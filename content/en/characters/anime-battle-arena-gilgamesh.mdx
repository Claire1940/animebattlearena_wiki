When Gilgamesh activates his transformation, "Pathetic Mongrels...", he takes his seat upon **Vimana: Throne of the Heaven-soaring King**. <Section icon={Shield} title="Advanced Tactics and Counter-Play">

Playing as Gilgamesh requires a "God Complex" mindset—you must control the pace of the fight and force the opponent to play your game. However, playing *against* him requires patience and an understanding of his cooldown windows. Most Gilgamesh players rely heavily on **Pinpoint Shot**; once that move is on cooldown, their offensive pressure drops significantly.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identify the M1 Pattern">
    Observe if the Gilgamesh player is using the "turning tech." If they are constantly spinning during M1s, avoid side-dashing and instead try to back-dash or jump-tilt to break their rhythm.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Bait the ZASSHU Counter">
    Never use your high-damage skills raw against a Gilgamesh with his counter up. Use a single M1 or a low-commitment projectile to bait the "Mongrel" voice line, then punish during his recovery frames.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Manage the Guardbreak Distance">
    **Exterminate** has a relatively short range (2.5 studs). By staying just outside of melee range, you can force him to whiff this move, leaving him open for a full counter-combo.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Evade Enuma Elish">
    When Gilgamesh awakens, watch for the Enkidu chains. If you get caught, you're likely dead. If you avoid the chains, stay at a distance. **Enuma Elish** has a yellow-range indicator; if you move side-to-side, it might catch you, but moving directly away usually works best.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Pro Insight">
High-ranking players often use "Lock-on" to manage Gilgamesh's projectiles. While some claim this is "brain dead" play, it is the most effective way to ensure **Pinpoint Shot** tracks correctly in high-lag environments.
</Callout>

</Section>
