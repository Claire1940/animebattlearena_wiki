**Detailed Move Analysis:**
- **Flame Strike**: Yamamoto dashes forward, leaving a trail of fire. If an enemy touches the fire, they are stunned, allowing for a follow-up.
- **Flashstep**: A quick dash followed by a flaming slash. This is your most reliable tool for breaking an opponent's guard.
- **Taimatsu**: A horizontal wave of fire. It serves as both a starter and an extender, reaching enemies at a moderate distance.
- **Incinerate**: A high-damage downward slash. Because it bypasses guard, it is perfect for finishing off defensive players.

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Combo Guide">

Since Yamamoto's M1s do not naturally combo, you must use his abilities to maintain a damage loop. Follow these steps to execute his most effective 2026 rotation.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Initiation">
    Start with **Taimatsu** to hit the enemy from a distance. This stuns them briefly and brings them into your melee range.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Guard Break">
    Immediately follow up with **Flashstep**. Since this is a guard break, it will hit even if the opponent recovers and tries to block the second hit.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Slam Down">
    Perform a **Down Slam** (M1 in the air) to ground the opponent. This sets up the perfect positioning for your fire trail.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Finisher">
    Use **Flame Strike** or **Incinerate** while they are grounded. Flame Strike is better if you want to keep them stunned, while Incinerate provides more raw damage.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Note">
In Bankai mode, your combo becomes much simpler: simply spam M1s in **East mode** to break guards, then finish with **North** once they are at low health.
</Callout>
