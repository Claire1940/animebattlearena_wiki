**Light Squids** serves as Geto's primary poke. It fires multiple squids that scale in damage based on distance. While he can turn during the initial wind-up, the second half of the move locks his orientation, making it vital to predict enemy movement.

**Taijutsu Combo** is his bread-and-butter melee string. If you time your clicks perfectly, the final kick deals less damage but prevents knockback, allowing for a seamless transition into another move. There is also a rare 1% chance to trigger a **Black Flash**, which adds significant bonus damage and visual flair.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Awakening: The Curse User's Transformation">

When Geto activates his awakening, he summons the **Rainbow Dragon** and **Kuchisake-Onna**, significantly increasing his threat level. His moveset shifts from tactical pokes to overwhelming AOE pressure and high-damage counters. The awakening soundtrack "With Rage" signals a shift in the match tempo that opponents must respect.

| Awakening Move | Damage | Type | Key Feature |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Zomba Barrage** | 45 Damage | Multi-Hit Slam | Summons 3 yellow beams |
| **Cyclops Punches** | 30 Damage | Guardbreak | Uppercut into Downslam |
| **Rainbow Dragon** | 21 Damage | Knockback | Can be used during hitstun |
| **Am I Pretty?** | 29 Damage | Counter | Ignores Guard entirely |

The **Rainbow Dragon** is arguably his most versatile awakening tool. Unlike most moves in **anime battle arena geto**'s kit, this can be activated while you are being hit (hitstun), serving as a powerful "get off me" tool. Once summoned, the dragon follows your cursor, allowing for complex zoning patterns.

**Am I Pretty?** features the Kuchisake-Onna spirit. This is a classic counter move; if Geto is struck while she is present, the opponent is trapped in a scissor-cutting animation that bypasses blocks. It is the perfect answer to aggressive rush-down characters.

<Callout type="tip" title="Mode Management">
Remember that **Stretchy Guard** in base form gives your opponent double mode gain. Only use it when absolutely necessary to avoid a lethal combo, as you don't want to accelerate their awakening faster than your own.
</Callout>

Positioning is everything for Geto. Because his **Light Squids** deal more damage at range, but his **Taijutsu Combo** requires melee contact, you must play a "footsies" game, constantly baiting the opponent into a range where you have the advantage.

Geto's **Stretchy Guard** is a double-edged sword. While it provides I-frames (Invincibility frames), the fact that it gives the enemy extra mode gain means you cannot spam it. In the 2026 meta, top-tier players only use this to dodge specific high-cooldown moves like ultimates or long-range stuns.

<Callout type="info" title="Range Scaling">
The damage for **Light Squids** ranges from 2.5 to 4 per hit across 8 hits. To hit the 32-damage cap, you need to be at least 20 studs away from your target. Practicing this distance in the training room is essential.
</Callout>
