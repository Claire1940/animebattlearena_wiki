<Section icon={Target} title="Core Mechanics of Ranged Combat">

To succeed with **anime battle arena ranged characters**, you must master three fundamental mechanics: spacing, projectile leading, and the "M1 to Skill" transition. Unlike melee characters who can mash Mouse1 to find a hit-stun, ranged characters must be deliberate with every input.

**Spacing and The Neutral Game**
Spacing is the distance you maintain between yourself and your opponent. For a ranged character, the goal is to stay just outside the enemy's M1 range while staying within your own skill range. This forces the opponent to use their dodge (Q) or a mobility skill to reach you. Once their defensive tools are on cooldown, you can unleash your high-damage projectiles.

**Projectile Leading**
Most projectiles in ABA have a travel time. You cannot simply aim where the enemy is; you must aim where they *will be*. This is especially true for characters like Yusuke or Sinon, whose primary damage comes from linear shots.

<Callout type="info" title="Pro Spacing Tip">
Use your Shift-lock to maintain a constant view of the opponent. This allows you to backpedal while firing, keeping the gap consistent.
</Callout>

| Combat Action | Ideal Range | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **M1 String** | Close | Damage / Combo extension |
| **Projectile Poke** | Mid-Long | Chipping health / Forcing block |
| **Guard Break** | Mid | Opening up defensive players |
| **Transformation** | Safe Distance | Powering up for the finish |

<Section icon={Shield} title="Counter-Play and Defensive Strategies">

Even as a ranged specialist, you will eventually face opponents who can close the gap. Knowing how to defend against rush-down characters is just as important as landing your shots.

**Defensive Tools**
- **Block (F)**: Essential for stopping predictable dash attacks or enemy projectiles.
- **Dodge (Q)**: Use this sparingly. A wasted dodge is an invitation for the enemy to start a 50%+ damage combo.
- **Counter Skills**: Some characters, like **Itachi** or **Aizen**, have counters built into their kits. Use these when you anticipate a dash-in.

| Opponent Type | Counter Strategy | Recommended Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Rush-down (Luffy/Zoro)** | Backpedal and Poke | Use knockback skills early |
| **Grapplers (Broly)** | Maintain Max Range | Dodge their grab at all costs |
| **Other Zoners** | Precision Trading | Wait for their cooldowns to end |
| **High Mobility (Minato)** | Bait and Punish | Block their initial dash, then M1 |

<Callout type="tip" title="Strategic Retreat">
If an enemy is pressuring you too hard, use your mobility skill (if available) to reset the fight. There is no shame in running to a better position to restart your zoning cycle.
</Callout>
