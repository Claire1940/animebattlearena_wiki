<Section icon={Map} title="Mastering 2v2 Combat Mechanics">

In **Anime Battle Arena (ABA)**, 2v2 matches are not just two simultaneous 1v1s. They are chaotic skirmishes where positioning is everything. You must constantly monitor your teammate's health and the cooldowns of both opponents. Wasting your dodge (Q) in a 2v2 is twice as dangerous as in a 1v1 because the second opponent is always looking for an opening to punish your lack of mobility.

| Action | Input | 2v2 Strategic Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sprint** | **Shift** | Use to reposition between both enemies/teammate |
| **Dodge** | **Q** | Save for escaping "team combos" where both hit you |
| **Block** | **F** | Essential for baiting out AOE skills from the enemy |
| **Basic Attack** | **M1** | Use to peel enemies off your teammate |
| **Transformation** | **G** | Activate when an opponent is low to finish the stock |

Understanding the different character archetypes is crucial for building a balanced team. You generally want one character who can "tank" or distract while the other deals the primary damage.

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step: Winning Your First 2v2 Match">

If you want to succeed with your **anime battle arena 2v2 characters**, you need a plan for the start of every round. Randomly throwing out special moves will only lead to your team being punished during cooldowns. Follow this structured approach to gain the upper hand early in the match.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Coordinate the Target">
    Before the match starts, decide which enemy to focus on first. Usually, you want to target the player with the lowest defensive utility or the one playing a high-threat "Legacy" character.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Initial Spacing and Baiting">
    Use **Shift** to move in and out of the enemy's range. Try to bait them into using their "Move 1" or "Move 2" and block it with **F**. Once they are on cooldown, they are vulnerable.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The First Confirm">
    Wait for your teammate to land a hit-stun move or a freeze. Immediately follow up with your fastest combo starter. This "cross-combo" is often impossible to dodge if timed correctly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Managing the Second Opponent">
    While you are comboing one enemy, keep an eye on the other. If they try to interrupt you, use your **Q Dodge** to reposition or have your teammate intercept them.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="The Awakening Push">
    When your **G meter** is full, coordinate your transformations. Using both awakenings at once can overwhelm the enemy team, but sometimes saving one for later is better for long-term pressure.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Pro Strategy">
Use the "Ladder" technique (Space + M1) to launch an enemy into the air. While they are airborne, they are much easier for your teammate to hit with a powerful special move.
</Callout>

</Section>
