<Section icon={Shield} title="Defensive Mechanics: Blocking and Dodging">

Defense is arguably more important than offense in high-level ABA play. The **anime battle arena controls** provide two main defensive tools: **F (Block)** and **Q (Dodge)**. Mastering the difference between these two is what separates beginners from ranked veterans. 

Blocking allows you to negate damage from most basic attacks and non-guard-breaking skills. However, many characters possess "Guard Breaks" (GBs) that will stun you if you hold the F key for too long. Dodging, mapped to the **Q** key, provides a brief window of invulnerability and repositioning but has a significant cooldown.

| Mechanic | Input | Benefit | Weakness |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Block** | **F** | Negates M1 damage and standard skills | Vulnerable to Guard Breaks and chip damage |
| **Dodge** | **Q** | Complete avoidance of an incoming attack | Long cooldown; leaves you open if mistimed |
| **Evasive** | **Varies** | Breaks an opponent's combo string | Usually requires a full meter or specific character |

  <Card title="When to Block (F)">
    - Against standard **Mouse1** strings.
    - When you see a multi-hit projectile approaching.
    - To bait an opponent into using a Guard Break you can dodge.
  </Card>
  <Card title="When to Dodge (Q)">
    - To escape a high-damage ultimate or Awakening skill.
    - When you are caught in a "Ladder" combo.
    - To reposition behind an opponent for a back-hit.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="warning" title="Cooldown Management">
Do not "spam" the **Q** key. If you waste your dodge while the opponent still has their high-damage skills available, you essentially hand them a free combo. Save your dodge for moves that you cannot reliably block.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Offensive Strings and Combo Execution">

The core of ABA's combat is the **Mouse1 (M1)** string. Most characters have a 4-hit or 5-hit basic attack sequence. The final hit usually results in a knockback, which ends your pressure. To maximize damage, players use **anime battle arena controls** to "extend" these combos using aerials and skills.

The most common extension is the **Uptilt**. By pressing **Space + Mouse1** during an M1 string, you launch the opponent into the air. This allows you to follow up with more hits or a special move while they are suspended, preventing them from blocking.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The M1 Opener">
    Land 3 consecutive **Mouse1** hits on the ground. This creates enough hit-stun to prevent the opponent from immediately dodging.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Aerial Launch">
    Press **Space + Mouse1** immediately after the third hit. This "Uptilt" launches the enemy into the air.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Air Follow-up">
    Follow the enemy into the air and land 2-3 more **Mouse1** hits. Do not finish the air string, or you will knock them away.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Skill Integration">
    Press **1, 2, 3, or 4** to use a special move while the enemy is still falling. This "confirms" the skill damage.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Combo Type | Input Sequence | Difficulty |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Basic Ground** | M1 > M1 > M1 > M1 | Beginner |
| **Uptilt Ladder** | M1 > M1 > M1 > Space+M1 > M1 > M1 | Intermediate |
| **Skill Confirm** | M1 > M1 > M1 > [Skill Key] | Intermediate |
| **Advanced Reset** | M1 > M1 > M1 > Q (behind) > M1 | Expert |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Combo Tip">
Characters like **Aoi Todo** or **Nanami** rely heavily on specific timing. Practice your M1 rhythms in a private server to ensure you aren't "mashing" the keys, which can lead to missed inputs and dropped combos.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Zap} title="Awakening and Transformation (G)">

The **G** key is the most powerful tool in your **anime battle arena controls** arsenal. As you deal and take damage, your yellow Awakening bar fills. Once full, pressing **G** transforms your character into their "Ultimate" form, granting new skills and increased damage.

Transformation is not just a power boost; it is a tactical reset. Activating your Awakening often provides a brief moment of invulnerability or a shockwave that pushes enemies back. This can be used defensively to stop an opponent's momentum or offensively to end a round quickly.

**Transformation Best Practices:**
- **Wait for the right moment**: Don't transform the second your bar is full if the opponent is already at low health. Save it for the next stock.
- **Identify the changes**: Some characters, like **Itadori** or **Aizen**, get completely different skill sets (1-4) during Awakening.
- **Watch the timer**: Awakening is temporary. If you spend the whole duration chasing a mobile opponent, you waste your greatest advantage.

| Action | Key | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Check Meter** | Visual | Look at the yellow bar at the bottom of the screen |
| **Activate** | **G** | Enter transformation mode; replaces skills 1-4 |
| **Ultimate Move** | **Varies** | Most awakened characters have a "one-shot" or high-damage move |

<Callout type="success" title="Winning with G">
Using **G** while your opponent's dodge (Q) is on cooldown is the most effective way to ensure your ultimate skills land. Always track their defensive usage before committing to your transformation.
</Callout>
