<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Fundamentals: M1s and Combo Theory">

In this **anime battle arena beginner guide**, understanding the "M1" system is paramount. Most characters rely on a four-hit Mouse1 string. The final hit of this string usually knocks the opponent away, ending your pressure. To maximize damage, advanced players use "uptilts" or "ladders" by jumping and attacking (Space + Mouse1) to keep the opponent in the air, preventing them from blocking.

Combat in ABA is a game of resources. You have health, energy for skills, and your dodge cooldown. If you use all your special moves at once and they are blocked, you are left vulnerable.

| Combat Phase | Primary Goal | Recommended Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Neutral** | Finding an opening | Poke with long-range skills or bait a dodge |
| **Advantage** | Maximizing damage | Use M1 strings into confirmed special moves |
| **Disadvantage** | Resetting the fight | Block, wait for a gap, then Q-Dodge away |
| **Awakening** | Closing the round | Use G-Transform to overwhelm the enemy |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Confirm the Hit">
    Never throw out a special move (1-4) randomly. Always start with a Mouse1 hit. If the M1 connects, the opponent is briefly stunned, making your special move "confirmed" or unblockable.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Extend with Aerials">
    After three M1 hits, jump and press M1 again. This "uptilt" launches the enemy. Follow them into the air for more hits to extend the combo duration.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Manage Cooldowns">
    Keep an eye on your skill icons. If your high-damage move is on cooldown, play defensively. Don't engage in a trade you can't win.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute Transformation">
    When your yellow bar is full, you can press G. This usually restores health and gives you a completely new, more powerful moveset. Use this when the opponent is at 50% health to secure a stock.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Dodge Management">
The Q-Dodge has a significant cooldown. If you dodge randomly, a skilled player will wait for the animation to end and then start a full combo that you cannot escape. Save your dodge for when you are trapped in a high-damage skill.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Ranked Play and PvP Strategy">

Once you feel comfortable with your character's moveset, it's time to enter Ranked 1v1. This is where the true test of this **anime battle arena beginner guide** lies. Ranked matches reward you with points that determine your standing in the community. To win consistently, you must transition from "button mashing" to "tactical fighting."

**Key PvP Strategies:**
- **Patience is Key**: Let the opponent attack first. Most beginners will spam their skills immediately. Block them, then punish during their recovery frames.
- **Guard Breaking**: Learn which of your skills can break blocks. Use these when an opponent is playing too defensively.
- **Environmental Awareness**: Don't get backed into a corner. Use the arena's space to keep your escape routes open.
- **Punishing the "G"**: If you see an opponent transform, play extremely safe. Their awakening is temporary; if you can run away or block until it ends, you've neutralized their biggest advantage.

| Feature | Casual Servers | Ranked 1v1 |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Player Count** | Up to 16 players | 2 players only |
| **Reward** | Fun and practice | Rank points and prestige |
| **Skill Level** | Mixed/New players | Competitive/High skill |
| **Map Size** | Large arenas | Small, focused maps |

<Callout type="info" title="Matchup Knowledge">
Winning in ABA is 50% mechanics and 50% knowledge. You need to know what the *other* characters do. If you don't know that a certain character has a counter-move, you will keep hitting them and taking damage.
</Callout>
