<Section icon={Shield} title="Defensive Strategies and Spacing">

In Anime Battle Arena, your defensive tools are just as important as your offensive ones. Beginners often exhaust their **Q (Dodge)** immediately after taking a single hit. This is a fatal error, as it leaves you vulnerable to a full combo extension. Effective spacing involves moving just outside the opponent's M1 range, forcing them to miss an attack, and then moving in to punish.

**Key Defensive Principles:**
- **Block First**: If an opponent is rushing you, hold **F**. Many special moves can be completely blocked, giving you a window to counter-attack.
- **Dodge with Purpose**: Only use **Q** to escape a "ladder" combo or a high-damage ultimate. If you dodge randomly, a skilled player will wait for the animation to end and punish you.
- **Resetting the Neutral**: If you find yourself low on health, use your sprint to create distance and wait for your special move cooldowns to refresh.

| Defensive Mechanic | Best Used Against | Common Mistake |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Static Blocking** | Multi-hit skills / M1 strings | Holding it too long and getting guard-broken. |
| **Directional Dodge** | Precision projectiles / Ults | Dodging into the opponent's next attack. |
| **Jump-Dashing** | Ground-based AOE skills | Wasting stamina without gaining distance. |

<Callout type="warning" title="Guard Breaking">
Be aware that certain special moves are "Guard Breaks." If you try to block these, your character will be stunned, leaving you open for a massive combo. Learn which characters have these moves to avoid being caught.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Zap} title="Building Your First Combos">

A "combo" in ABA is a sequence of attacks that prevents the opponent from escaping. The most basic combo every beginner must learn is the **M1 String into a Special Move**. Most characters allow for four consecutive M1 hits before the opponent is knocked away. To maximize damage, you should interrupt the fourth hit with a special skill or an aerial launcher.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The M1 Confirm">
    Approach the enemy and land 3 consecutive Mouse1 hits. Do not click the 4th time yet, as that will trigger a knockback.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Uplift (Aerial)">
    Instead of the 4th M1, press **Space + Mouse1** simultaneously. This launches the opponent into the air.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Aerial Follow-up">
    While in the air, land another 2-3 M1 hits. This keeps the opponent in a "hit-stun" state where they cannot block.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Skill Finisher">
    Before falling to the ground, use your **1, 2, or 3 skill**. This ensures the skill lands while the opponent is unable to dodge.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Combo Type | Key Sequence | Difficulty |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Basic Ground** | M1 x3 -> Skill 1 | Very Easy |
| **Simple Ladder** | M1 x3 -> Space+M1 -> M1 x2 -> Skill 2 | Medium |
| **Awakened Burst** | G -> Skill 1 -> M1 x3 -> Skill 4 | Easy (Post-G) |

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Ranked Progression and PvP Habits">

Ranked 1v1 is where you will truly test your skills. Unlike casual servers, ranked players will punish every missed move. To climb the ladder in 2026, you must stop "throwing" moves and start "fishing" for openings.

**Pro Habits for Ranked:**
- **Patience over Aggression**: Let the opponent attack your block. Once they finish their string, they are in "recovery," which is your chance to strike.
- **Resource Management**: Watch your Awakening meter. If you are at 90%, play defensively until you can transform and end the round.
- **Matchup Knowledge**: Learn the cooldowns of popular characters. If you know Gojo just used his "Red" skill, you have a 10-15 second window where he is less dangerous.
