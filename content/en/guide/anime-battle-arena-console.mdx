<Section icon={Swords} title="Xbox Controller Layout & Button Mapping">

To compete at a high level, you must memorize the default controller layout. Unlike PC players who use a keyboard, console players have their skills mapped directly to the face buttons and triggers. Memorizing these inputs ensures you do not waste crucial defensive options like your dodge or block during intense pressure.

| Action | Xbox Input | PlayStation Equivalent | Purpose & Tactical Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Basic Attack (M1)** | **B Button** | Circle | Start combos, apply close-range hit-stun, and initiate ladders. |
| **Jump / Lift** | **A Button** | Cross | Launch into the air or extend your combo into an aerial string. |
| **Dodge / Shift-Lock** | **L3 (Left Stick Click)** | L3 | Quick dash to escape pressure or reposition during neutral play. |
| **Block** | **LT (Left Trigger)** | L2 | Defend against incoming basic attacks and non-guardbreak skills. |
| **Lock-On Target** | **R3 (Right Stick Click)** | R3 | Automatically track your opponent with the camera (Console exclusive). |
| **Special Move 1** | **X Button** | Square | Execute your character's first signature offensive or utility skill. |
| **Special Move 2** | **Y Button** | Triangle | Execute your second skill, often a combo extender or projectile. |
| **Special Move 3** | **RB (Right Bumper)** | R1 | Execute your third skill, typically a high-damage utility move. |
| **Special Move 4** | **LB (Left Bumper)** | L1 | Execute your fourth skill, which frequently acts as a guardbreak. |
| **Awakening (G)** | **DPad Down** | DPad Down | Activate your character's ultimate transformation when the bar is full. |

<Callout type="warning" title="Important">
The Lock-On feature (R3) is incredibly powerful for tracking fast-moving opponents, but do not rely on it when using manual-aim projectile skills (like certain beam attacks). Lock-on can sometimes cause projectiles to fire slightly behind a moving target.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="How to Master Aerial Ladder Combos on Controller">

The defining factor of a skilled ABA player is their ability to "ladder"—launching an opponent into the air and executing a series of basic attacks (M1s) without letting them fall to the ground. On an **anime battle arena console** setup, performing a ladder requires a slightly different physical approach than on PC.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initiate the Grounded M1 Chain">
    Approach your opponent and land your first three basic attacks on the ground using the **B Button**. Do not complete the full 4-hit string yet, as the final hit will knock them away.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Launch with Space / Jump">
    On your fourth strike, hold down the **A Button** (Jump) while pressing the **B Button** (Attack). This launches both your character and the opponent upward into the air.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Hold the Attack Button">
    Instead of repeatedly tapping the attack button in the air, hold down the **B Button** and the **A Button** simultaneously. This ensures your character climbs with the opponent and prevents accidental drop-offs.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Delay Your Mid-Air Strikes">
    Wait approximately 0.5 seconds between your aerial strikes to prevent triggering an early knockback animation. This delay keeps the opponent suspended in a hit-stun loop.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Finish with a Special Move">
    Before the gravity scaling drops your opponent to the floor, cancel your final aerial strike into one of your face-button special moves (such as **X** or **Y**) to maximize your damage output.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Skill Check">
Practice your ladder timing on the training dummies in a private server first. If you trigger an early knockback that sends the dummy flying horizontally, you are pressing your attack buttons too quickly without waiting for the 0.5-second delay.
</Callout>

</Section>
